Trauma-Informed, Neurodiversity-Informed Inclusive Design
Could a trauma-informed, neurodiversity-informed human–AI play interaction help self-disclosure and healing of survivors who kept silence, creating ripple effect like #MeToo did?

I am a practice-based HCI researcher and designer exploring the intersection of neurodiversity, complex trauma, and community-based participatory design for education, mental health care, and forensic interviewing. My work focuses on trauma-informed and neurodiversity-informed inclusive design, investigating pathways to post-trauma recovery and empowerment of child and adult survivors. I create play-based tangible toolkits using physical computing, XR, and digital fabrication technologies, embracing digital-physical hybridity to enable safe, sensory-rich, and embodied engagement.

You Will Know Me: From Silence to Story

A multi-sensory interactive puzzle combining physical wooden components with digital interaction, designed through participatory co-design for trauma-informed healing and empowerment.

Wish You Were Here XR: Invisible Emergencies

Interactive Augmented Reality experience connected with physical stimuli, designed as an accessible XR-based tool for managing sensory overload and trauma-related crises.

Pruney Fingers: A Tale of Us

A multi-sensory, trans-media, and interactive storybook where you see the world through the eyes of a brave and curious girl hitting puberty while dealing with her trauma. She is conducting her very first research and it's rocky.
