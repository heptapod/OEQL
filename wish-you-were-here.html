Wish You Were Here: Psychological and Sensory First Aid for Invisible Emergencies in Trauma and Neurodiversity

Keywords: ethical design, affective interaction, tangible interface, psychological first aid (PFA), sensory assistive technology, bodily playfulness, sensory XR

Related Projects: [You Will Know Me] [Pruney Fingers: An Old Tale of Us]

Question

Could we create a sense of safety by combining tangible, embodied interaction with Augmented Reality when we need this sensation?

The Problem: Invisible Emergencies

Individuals with PTSD, CPTSD, ASD, and ADHD encounter unpredictable emotional and sensory triggers in everyday environments—loud noises, bright lights, crowded spaces—that remain invisible yet deeply disruptive to maintaining a sense of safety.

Solution: Wish You Were Here, an Emergency Toolkit

1. Portable, Wearable, Tangible Interface

A one-minute experience combining DIY Google Cardboard, a smartphone, a soft touchable object, and paper markers. This affordable, accessible system delivers psychological first aid through tangible contact and immediate AR support.

2. Multi-Sensory Affective Interaction

Inspired by Harry Harlow's Contact Comfort studies, the interaction combines physical touch with an augmented 2D face that responds with progressive smiles (Default → Small Smile → Big Smile Ending). This three-state feedback reinforces user agency and provides non-verbal communication for those in sensory or emotional crisis.

3. Trauma-Informed Design for Real-World Use

Audio is intentionally excluded; ear covers and earplugs support sensory regulation. Minimal setup enables immediate deployment in public transit, crowded spaces, and unexpected moments of distress.

Future Work

I am continuing to develop this project with plans to incorporate olfactory interactions through perception engineering approaches, aiming to create more robust multi-sensory interventions for creating perceived safety.
