Yeonhee Choi

I'm a researcher and designer specializing in therapeutic human-computer play interaction based on participatory, co-design approaches with end users. I led three ethical design projects with a focus on fairness and inclusion of child and adult survivors of childhood trauma as well as people with neurodiversity caring intersectional vulnerability, finding interdisciplinary solutions for unmet real-world needs, especially the emotional needs.

I'm currently a MA Playable Experience Design student in Computing Department, Goldsmiths college, University of London. I'm expected to graduate in 2026 with Distinction, and am a recipient of Gold Award and Santander Piloting and Testing Grant. My MA thesis project, You Will Know Me was selected and presented at three conferences with different domains; art, interaction design, and medicine.

My multi-sensory storytelling project Pruney Fingers selected for funding and work space at Pan-Gyo Techno Valley. I'm currently developing Wish You Were Here XR, a portable and wearable assistive technology for sensory difficulties of ASD and people with PTSD/CPTSD.

Projects

You Will Know Me: From Silence to Story

A multi-sensory interactive puzzle combining physical wooden components with digital interaction, designed through participatory co-design for trauma-informed healing and empowerment.

Wish You Were Here XR: Invisible Emergencies

Interactive Augmented Reality experience connected with physical stimuli, designed as an accessible XR-based tool for managing sensory overload and trauma-related crises.

Pruney Fingers: A Tale of Us

Access to information of my body, denied. Is representation of female body in EDSA contents injurious to children's well being?
